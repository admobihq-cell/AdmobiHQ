# Admobi

## Register

**brand** — The public marketing site is the primary surface; it must sell digital taxi-top OOH to advertisers and recruit fleet partners.

## Product purpose

Admobi sells **LED taxi-top advertising** in Kenya that is **geotargeted** and **schedule- and creative-flexible**. The product helps brands and organizations reach people in motion across cities with short flight times (including one-day minimums) and measurable storytelling compared to static roadside inventory alone.

## Users

- **Primary buyers:** Business owners and marketing leads at SMEs, corporates, event organizers, and campaign teams (including election-related campaigns where permitted).
- **Fleet side:** Fleet managers who install and monetize units, and drivers who carry the hardware.
- **Context:** Busy decision-makers comparing channels on short timelines; they need clarity on reach, process, and credibility.

## Brand personality

- **Bold SaaS:** Confident product language, strong primary actions, clean structure.
- **Trustworthy:** Plain statements, no hype, room for compliance and specs as you scale.
- **Urban Kenyan:** Nairobi-first, with a credible path to more cities.

## Anti-references

- Generic “OOH dashboard” dark blue hero templates and stock “big number + tiny label” SaaS heroes.
- Neon crypto aesthetic, gradient text, decorative glass cards, and left-edge accent stripes on every block.
- Named competitor attacks on the homepage; keep differentiation factual and category-level unless you add a dedicated comparison page later.

## Strategic design principles

1. **Mobility over monolith** — Lead with taxis and routes, not abstract “impressions” alone.
2. **Two front doors** — Campaign leads and fleet partnerships are equal primary jobs; waitlist and driver signup stay visible but secondary.
3. **Placeholder honesty** — Numeric proof uses clearly labeled illustrative figures until production data exists.
4. **English first** — Copy in English for v1; structure leaves room for Swahili later.

## Competitive landscape (context only)

Static billboards and broader OOH networks (including digital taxi-top operators in-market) are alternatives. Admobi’s story is **moving, addressable screens** with geo and time controls and low minimums for tests and bursts.

## Accessibility and content

- Legible type, sufficient contrast, semantic structure for forms and FAQs.
- Footer can host privacy and terms as they are drafted.
