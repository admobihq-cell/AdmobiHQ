# Admobi AI SEO — Phase 2 content roadmap

Phase 1 (machine-readable files, schema, `/pricing`, on-page structure) ships without a CMS. Use this document when you are ready to scale editorial content for AI citation and topical authority.

## CMS direction (choose one)

| Option | Best when |
|--------|-----------|
| **MDX / Contentlayer in-repo** | Developers publish 10–20 guides; lowest operational overhead |
| **Sanity or Payload** | Marketing publishes weekly without deploys for copy-only changes |

Requirements for any CMS:

- Visible `datePublished` / `dateModified` on articles
- Author or team attribution (real names)
- FAQ and HowTo blocks where relevant
- Canonical URLs and sitemap integration

## Priority content backlog

| Priority | Type | Target query fan-out | Notes |
|----------|------|----------------------|-------|
| P1 | Definitive guide | digital OOH Kenya, taxi top advertising Kenya | One comprehensive pillar page |
| P1 | How-to | how to plan one-day taxi-top campaign Nairobi | Step structure for HowTo schema |
| P2 | Category comparison | moving LED taxi tops vs static billboards Nairobi | No named competitor attacks ([PRODUCT.md](../PRODUCT.md)) |
| P3 | City pages | Nakuru, Mombasa OOH | Publish only when inventory is live — avoid thin city pages |

## Third-party presence (parallel track)

- Kenyan marketing and OOH trade publications (guest posts, roundups)
- Authentic participation in relevant Reddit / LinkedIn threads
- Google Business Profile and local listings kept current
- Wikipedia only if standard notability criteria are met

## What not to do

- Separate “AI-only” content variants (Google scaled-content policy risk)
- Named competitor attack pages unless you add a dedicated, factual comparison URL
- Fabricated statistics or fake expert quotes
- Blocking GPTBot, PerplexityBot, or ClaudeBot if you want citations
